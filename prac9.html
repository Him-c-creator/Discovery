Insane planets in the Universe

Kepler 16b

Kepler 16B is a circumbinary planet located approximately 200 light-years away in the constellation Cygnus. It orbits two stars, making it a unique example of a planet in a binary star system.

HD189733b

HD 189733 b is a hot Jupiter exoplanet located approximately 63 light-years away in the constellation Vulpecula. It orbits a sun-like star and is known for its atmospheric properties and high temperature.

J1407b

J1407b is a young exoplanet located approximately 170 light-years away in the constellation Ophiuchus. It is notable for its massive ring system, which is much larger than Saturn's rings.

Kepler 186f

Kepler 186f is an exoplanet located approximately 149 light-years away in the constellation Cygnus. It is a super-Earth that orbits within the habitable zone of its star.

PSO j318.5-22

PSO J318.5−22 is a young, free-floating "rogue" planet located roughly 80 light-years away, discovered in 2013. It does not orbit a star and has an estimated mass of about 8.3 Jupiter masses.
